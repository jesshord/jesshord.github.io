My name is Jessica Hord. I'm on my second year of my Masters degree (of three, I'm part-time). I currently work at T-Mobile as a UX designer full-time. Previously, I worked full-time as a Visual Designer.

In terms of digital fabrication, I'm most interested in physical prototyping and physical product design. A1:

Assignment 1 was to make a kit consisting of at least 30 instances of at least one unique part. The parts must have slots that press fit into the other parts in the kit. The parts must be laser cut out of cardboard.

A2:

Assignment 2 was to create a parametric model using a grasshopper definition. The parts should be able to be clipped together. At least one of the parts should connect a piece of cardboard to another piece of cardboard.

A3:

Assignment 3 was to assemble your 3D printer, adjust plate level, and design/build 4 cubes, 2 tubes, 4 cylinders, and one 3 part nested object.

A4:

Assignment 4 was to design a box that is made up of flat panels with fingerjoints/boxjoints, tabs, or another joining method design for sheet material. It should have an interference fit.


A5:

Assignment 5 was to combine two STLs in Rhino in a way that passes all rapid prototyping tests.

A6:

Assignment 6 was to create a lamp.
